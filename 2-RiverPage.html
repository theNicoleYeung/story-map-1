Mura-Drava-Danube Biosphere

The three rivers - Mura, Drava, and Danube - flow through and connect Austria, Croatia, Hungary, Serbia, and Slovenia. The Mura-Drava-Danube system spans 700 kilometers in length and 930,000 hectares in size, forming a vital artery of the Green Heart that traverses across floodplain forests, sandbanks, and river islands.

The extraordinary biodiversity in these riverine areas are unmatched, comparable only to tropical rainforests, hence granting this biosphere the title “the Amazon of Europe”.

Tale of the Otter

Eurasian River Otter
(Lutra lutra)

In the heart of Europe, where the mighty rivers intertwine, the Eurasian Otter thrives.

The pristine rivers and expansive wetlands provide its every necessity — crystal-clear waters brimming with fishes, cozy nooks along the riverbanks for shelter, and a bustling community of fellow fauna that call this place home.

Known for its playful nature and graceful mastery in the waterways, you can often recognize the otter as it dives into the cool depths - a sleek silhouette against the shimmering rapids, navigating the labyrinth of currents with effortless agility.

The otter is a testament to the resilience and vitality of this beating heart of Europe.

Tale of the Tern

Little Tern
(Sternula albifrons)

Along the coast of the European Amazon, where sandy shores meet the ebb and flow of the river tides, you can catch glimpses of the spirited dance of the Little Tern.

This tiny seabird - smallest of its kind in Europe in fact - returns to its summer home each year, as the ocean breeze carries whispers of warmer days.

Amongst the sun-kissed sands and gentle murmurs of estuaries, the Little Tern - adorned with a black crown on top of its white forehead - seeks out the perfect nesting grounds to build its family.

This is where it brings its young to the world.

Tale of the Eagle

White-tailed Eagle
(Haliaeetus albicilla)

Among the towering trees and winding rivers, the White-tailed Eagle soars with grace and strength. The Amazon of Europe is its kingdom, a realm where its every wingbeat echoes the heartbeat of the land.

As a young eagle, it has learned to hunt from its parents, mastering the art of diving gracefully into the water to snatch fish with its powerful talons. Its keen eyesight could spot a fish shimmering beneath the surface from great heights, a skill honed through generations of wisdom.

As an apex predator and an adept fisher, the White-tailed Eagle plays an indispensable role in upholding the delicate balance of the food chain
- this is the guardian of the rivers for all.

When the rivers flow, the symphony of life harmonizes and resonates across the land and sea.

But - this vital riverine shelter is in danger.

The natural flow and dynamics of these rivers have been disrupted.

Hydropower construction

River channeling and engineering

Destruction of natural flood-plains

Out-dated water management infrastructure and practices have significantly disrupted the freshwater ecosystem, impairing important habitats and disconnecting aquatic species from their home. Danube Salmon and Sturgeons, for example, cannot reach their spawning grounds and have been pushed closer to extinction. In fact, two of the six wild sturgeon species have gone extinct in recent decades.

In addition, habitat fragmentation and degradation have been exacerbated by the compounding factors of pollutants from agricultural run-offs and industrial spills, deforestation, and climate change.

When the rivers are gone, so are the rhythms of life.
